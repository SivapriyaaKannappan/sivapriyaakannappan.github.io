About Me

I am a research scientist and a software developer at VisionMetric Ltd., where I develop commercial products for our company incorporating deep learning, image processing and pattern recognition techniques. My research interests lie in computer vision, image processing, pattern recognition,machine learning, deep learning, artificial intelligence, website design and software engineering.

Projects

Advanced value of sensor data in cycling through machine learning

This project focuses on accurately determining a rider's position by integrating computer vision tracking algorithms with motion capture (MC) camera data. The system classifies the rider's pose, helping to validate results from body rocket sensor data. By evaluating various datasets against MC camera data, the goal is to identify the most effective pose estimation algorithm. Once the optimal algorithm is selected, it will be used to extract key points from the riderâ€™s movements and classify their four time-trial (TT) positions using deep learning techniques. This approach aims to improve precision in tracking and classifying rider positions, ultimately enhancing performance analysis and training outcomes.

Key-frame Analysis and Extraction for Automatic Summarization of Real-time Videos

Developed automatic video summarization and evaluation techniques for effective and efficient key frame retrieval. Compared existing summarization methods and provide a new ranking based on their performance, as well as investigated the best performance an automatic video summarization can achieve.

Enhancing Information Security through Digital Image Steganography by combining both Spatial and Frequency Domain

Steganography is meant for concealed writing in which the secret information (can be in any form such as text, image, audio or video) can be hidden in any kind of carrier medium (such as text, image, audio or video). Highlighted a technique of combining AES algorithm with Least Significant Bit (LSB) substitution in Spatial domain and Discrete Wavelet Transform (DWT) in Frequency domain approach of steganography, in order to achieve better image quality and high level of security.
